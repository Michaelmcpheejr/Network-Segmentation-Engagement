## What This Engagement Demonstrates

This engagement demonstrates the ability to assess network segmentation across switching, routing, and firewall layers while identifying real-world lateral movement risks in a multi-segment environment.

Through configuration analysis and stakeholder interview validation, this assessment highlights how segmentation can exist in design but fail in enforcement, resulting in a flat internal trust model.

Key outcomes include:
- Identification of segmentation control gaps across VLAN design, trunk configurations, and firewall policy
- Validation of technical findings through direct engineer interview and intent vs. implementation comparison
- Translation of technical misconfigurations into business risk, including data exposure and lack of defense-in-depth
- Application of industry frameworks (NIST 800-53, NIST CSF, CIS Controls, MITRE ATT&CK) to real-world scenarios
- Development of actionable remediation strategies to reduce lateral movement exposure and improve overall security posture
